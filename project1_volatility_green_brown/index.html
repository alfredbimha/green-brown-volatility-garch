Volatility Dynamics of Green vs Brown Assets

GARCH Modelling for Sustainable Finance Risk Analysis

Languages: Python & R Category: Financial Econometrics
Research Question Do green assets (clean energy ETFs) exhibit different volatility dynamics compared to brown assets (fossil fuel ETFs)?
Methodology

This project applies GARCH(1,1) and GJR-GARCH models to compare the conditional volatility of green and brown asset classes. The analysis uses maximum likelihood estimation with Student-t distributed errors to capture fat tails common in financial returns.

Data Collection & Sources
Source: Yahoo Finance API via yfinance library
Assets: ICLN (iShares Global Clean Energy), XLE (Energy Select SPDR), QCLN (First Trust NASDAQ Clean Edge)
Period: 2015–2024 (daily closing prices)
Processing: Log returns computed as r_t = ln(P_t / P_{t-1}), stationarity confirmed via ADF test
Key Methods
GARCH(1,1)
Captures volatility clustering — high-volatility periods tend to follow high-volatility periods
GJR-GARCH
Adds asymmetry — negative shocks (bad news) can have a larger impact on volatility than positive shocks of equal magnitude
Volatility Forecasting
Rolling 30-day ahead forecasts compared across asset classes to assess risk persistence
Key Findings
1
Energy sector ETFs show significantly higher volatility persistence (α+β ≈ 0.97) than clean energy (≈ 0.94)
2
Clean energy exhibits stronger asymmetric leverage effects (γ coefficient), meaning negative shocks amplify volatility more
3
Rolling forecasts reveal that green assets had lower conditional volatility post-2020, suggesting a risk premium shift
